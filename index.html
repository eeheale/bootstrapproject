Intro

I have always enjoyed capturing moments and time periods with photographs. Seeing a photograph takes me back to the experience surrounding when the photo was taken more than memory does on its own. When I see a photograph I am often reminded of memories that are stored away somewhere in my head. They may not have come out again without seeing the photographs.

Unfortunately, about a year ago I deleted most of the thousands of photos and videos of my life that I had taken. The pictures I've included in this website are ones that survived from either being stored on my computer or found somewhere else.

2018

The year of 2018 was a very exciting and memorable year for me. It was the year I graduated highschool and a few months later, moved into the dorms in Harrisonburg to start college at JMU. I had long awaited graduating high school and the summer after just graduating gave me a new sense of freedom and release

2019

2019 was possibly even more monumental than 2018. I had made many friends living in the dorms and began to feel part of a community. I traveled by plane alone for the first time to visit my friend for spring break. It was also my first time on the west coast, the friend I was visiting lived in Portland, Oregon. In July, I adopted a kitten from the local animal shelter and named her Ivy. Having her has been one of the best things to happen to me. In August, I moved into an apartment for the first time with a friend from my dorm and two other girls. This was truly life changing for me. I met more friends this year and things seemed to continue to open up for me in a way they hadn't before.

2020

A few months after 2020 began, the pandemic caused lockdowns and quarentine to began. Despite these drastic life changes and events, this time was extremely peaceful and therapeutic for me. I felt connected to my peers because many people my age were more active on social media due to the pandemic. The large number of people experiencing the same thing and the use of humour and entertainment was big during this time. I had more time for myself and much more flexibility with my daily schedule. I would facetime and talk with friends more often. The overall theme of this time period was a beautiful balance of peace and chaos.

2021

In 2021, I was living in another apartment and had my own bathroom for the first time. This is something I had always wanted. Growing up I shared a bathroom with two brothers. I ended up getting a beautiful view outside my bedroom window that I still miss to this day. I could see trees, a field, a windy road up the mountain, and houses in the distance. It was extra beautiful in the summer when all the greenery was swaying in full bloom. I spent most of my time here with online clssses and the pandemic.
